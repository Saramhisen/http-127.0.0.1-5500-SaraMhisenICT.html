Work and Energy

Explain the relationship between work and energy.

Energy

Energy is the ability of a system to do work. This means that the system can change itself or the world around it. A joule, is the amount of energy used when a force of one newton (1 N) is applied over a distance of one meter (1 m). 1 J = 1 N × 1 m = 1 N.m

Work-Energy Theorem

The work-energy theorem states that the work done (W) on a system is equal to the change in energy (ΔE) of the system. W = ΔE

Kinetic Energy

When you do work on an object, you give it energy. For example, when you heat something up, you give it thermal energy. When you lift something up, you give it gravitational potential energy.

Kinetic energy (KE) is the energy generally associated with motion.
